Royal Palace

The majestic Royal Palace, built in the 18th century, serves as the official residence for state ceremonies and is one of Madrid's most iconic landmarks.

Retiro Park

Retiro Park is a beautiful and expansive green space in the heart of Madrid, featuring gardens, sculptures, and the famous Crystal Palace.

Plaza Mayor

This historic square, once a marketplace and festival venue, is surrounded by picturesque buildings and lively cafés.

Prado Museum

One of the world's most renowned art museums, the Prado houses masterpieces by Velázquez, Goya, and other European artists.

Gran Vía

Gran Vía is Madrid's bustling main street, known for its shops, theaters, and iconic early 20th-century architecture.

Puerta de Alcalá

Built in 1778, Puerta de Alcalá is a grand neoclassical gateway and one of Madrid's most recognized landmarks, located at the entrance to Retiro Park.

Almudena Cathedral

The Almudena Cathedral is a stunning modern church with neo-Gothic architecture, completed in 1993 and situated next to the Royal Palace.

San Miguel Market

San Miguel Market is a historic and vibrant market offering a wide selection of Spanish delicacies, fresh produce, and gourmet treats in the heart of Madrid.

Temple of Debod

The Temple of Debod is an ancient Egyptian temple gifted to Spain, offering a unique historical experience and spectacular sunset views over the city.
