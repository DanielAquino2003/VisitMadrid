Top Sightseeing Spots

Royal Palace

The grand Royal Palace, built in the 18th century, serves as the official residence for state ceremonies and is one of Madrid's iconic landmarks.

El Retiro Park

El Retiro Park is a beautiful and expansive green space in the heart of Madrid, offering gardens, sculptures, and the famous Crystal Palace.

Plaza Mayor

This historic square, once a marketplace and site for festivals, is surrounded by picturesque buildings and vibrant cafes.

El Prado Museum

One of the most renowned art museums in the world, El Prado hosts masterpieces by Velázquez, Goya, and other European artists.

Gran Vía

Gran Via is Madrid's bustling main street, known for its shopping, theaters, and iconic architecture dating back to the early 20th century.

Puerta Alcalá

Built in 1778, Puerta Alcalá is a grand neoclassical gate and one of Madrid's most recognized monuments, standing at the entrance to El Retiro Park.

Almudena Cathedral

The Almudena Cathedral is a beautiful and modern church with neo-Gothic architecture, completed in 1993 and located next to the Royal Palace.

San Miguel Market

San Miguel Market is a vibrant, historic market offering a wide selection of Spanish delicacies, fresh produce, and gourmet treats in the heart of Madrid.
