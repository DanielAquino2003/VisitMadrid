Foundation

9th Century

Madrid was founded as a fortress in the 9th century by Emir Muhammad I of Córdoba.

Modern Era

16th Century

In the 16th century, Philip II made Madrid the capital of Spain.

Arab Domination and Influence

9th-11th Centuries

Under Muslim rule, Madrid was known as "Mayrit" and was primarily a military fortress. Its influence can still be seen in some architectural remains and place names.

The Habsburg Era

16th-17th Centuries

During the 16th and 17th centuries, the Habsburg dynasty enriched Madrid with notable monuments, including Plaza Mayor and the Royal Palace. This period is often referred to as the "Madrid of the Austrias."

Spanish War of Succession

Early 18th Century

In the early 18th century, Madrid was significantly affected by the Spanish War of Succession, leading to the rise of the Bourbon dynasty and modernizing reforms.

Spanish Civil War

1936-1939

Madrid played a pivotal role during the Spanish Civil War (1936-1939), enduring a long siege and witnessing significant battles that shaped the country's future.

Post-Franco Democracy

1975-Present

After Francisco Franco's death in 1975, Madrid became the heart of Spain's transition to democracy, hosting political movements and cultural changes that transformed the nation.

Modern Madrid

Present Day

Today, Madrid is a vibrant metropolis and cultural center, blending historical heritage with contemporary architecture and a cosmopolitan lifestyle.
